Major research papers
Trading strategies with code
I'm excited to share with you a curated database of trading systems based on academic papers. It has a variety of strategies, including market timing, trend following, asset allocation, and stock picking, across equities, bonds, commodities, and FX.
Here's how I create these strategies: I continually build a database of quantitative trading ideas derived from academic research papers. I read through hundred of papers, select the best ones, and extract trading rules in plain language, performance and risk characteristics. The selected strategies are then added to this page.
I provide an implementation of selected strategies in Backtrader's framework.
You may wonder why academics would publish a working strategy. Academics often do so to show they have bright ideas, to climb the academic career ladder, or to start a side business as a consultant for a hedge fund or start a fund on their own. Asset management companies publish working trading strategies to raise more assets and gain the trust of pension and sovereign funds and family offices.
Another common question is whether a published strategy will stop working. Multiple research papers have shown that while the strategy may be less profitable than in the past and have a lower return-to-risk ratio, it can still be part of a diversified multi-strategy portfolio. Market anomalies are not easy to arbitrage, and most have time-varying returns, making them less risk-free.
