Stock Classification Impact: Enhancing Trading Strategies
A python Backtrader implementation of the algorithmic trading strategy described in the following paper.

Trading rules
Investment universe: NYSE, AMEX, and NASDAQ-traded stocks
Official industry return: Equally weighted return of peers based on SIC classification
Fundamental industry return: Equally weighted return of fundamental peers identified by Hoberg and Phillips methodology
Hoberg and Phillips (HP) industry classifications: Constructed using product descriptions from 10K forms and word vectors to compute product similarity
HP classifications source: http://www.rhsmith.umd.edu/industrydata/index.html
Weekly stock sorting: Five quintiles based on the difference between official and fundamental industry returns during the previous week
Q1: Stocks with the most negative difference between official and fundamental industry returns (bottom 20%)
Q5: Stocks with the most positive difference between official and fundamental industry returns (top 20%)
Q1-Q5 portfolio: Long in Q1 stocks, short in Q5 stocks
Portfolio weighting: Equally weighted
Rebalancing frequency: Weekly
Why it works
Academic research reveals that firms have strong co-movement with their official industry and weak co-movement with their fundamental peers at high frequencies, like weekly, while the opposite is true at lower frequencies. This reversal supports the hypothesis that bounded rationality causes some agents to overcompensate for official industry shocks in the short-term. These mistakes are then corrected over time, leading to a change in co-movement patterns.
Hedging stocks in bear markets?
The source and related research papers do not provide information on the correlation between the proposed trading strategy and equity market risk, making it unclear if the strategy can be used for hedging or diversification during market crises. The long-short strategy can be divided into two parts, with the long leg likely having a strong correlation to the equity market. The short-only leg could potentially be used as a hedge during downturns. However, a thorough backtest is necessary to determine the return/risk characteristics and correlation of the strategy.
