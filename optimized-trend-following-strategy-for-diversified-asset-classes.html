Trading rules
Invest in 5 equal-weighted ETFs: SPY (US stocks), EFA (foreign stocks), BND (bonds), VNQ (REITs), and GSG (commodities). Hold an ETF if its price is above its 10-month Simple Moving Average. Otherwise, keep the allocation in cash.
Why it works
The strategy's concept is simple: use momentum or trend-following filters to identify periods of low performance with high volatility and periods of high performance with low volatility. If successful, this approach can outperform a passive buy-and-hold strategy by effectively timing individual asset classes, resulting in better returns and lower drawdowns.
By creating a diverse portfolio of several asset classes with built-in momentum filters, investors can exploit the diversification benefits of low correlation between assets. This allows them to remain invested in suitable asset classes while avoiding undesirable ones. Since 1973, using this strategy, an investor could have increased risk-adjusted returns, diversified their portfolio, and implemented a market-timing solution. This would have helped them avoid many bear markets associated with different asset classes, achieving equity-like returns with bond-like volatility and drawdown.
As Collie, Sylvanus, and Thomas mention in "Volatility-responsive asset allocation," market volatility is variable, with periods of stability and explosive volatility. Therefore, the risk of a traditional fixed-weight strategic asset allocation policy can also be highly variable over time.
Hedging stocks in bear markets?
Tactical asset allocation strategies, such as Mebane Faber's "A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation," typically involve rotating out of equity-like risk assets during times of stress. These strategies don't directly hedge equity risk as an add-on to a portfolio. Instead, they serve as an overlay to manage the proportion of equities or equity-like assets in a portfolio. By doing so, they can reduce the overall risk associated with equities and enhance risk-adjusted returns.
