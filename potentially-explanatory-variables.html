Perform a multi-scale decomposition for these variables according to three types of scales (calendar/trade/volumes) and conduct a descriptive analysis of their behavior.
Financial data has three main types of non-linearity:
time-varying relationships
variable interactions
non-linear shapes like y=x2y = x^2y=x2ï»¿
Time-variation is generally more significant than other factors, so it's common to use a window of recent data and discard older points. This approach can make it difficult to detect other factors and avoid overfitting. To address this, using a time-varying linear model is a reasonable choice. In trading, first-order effects (e.g., returns) are usually the main focus, while higher-order effects like skewness or kurtosis are less significant but can be interesting from a risk perspective. If you want to include a specific variable interaction or non-linear shape like y=x2y = x^2y=x2ï»¿, you can add another variable that is the product of the desired variables or generated by the formula. Machine learning achieves these effects automatically but with less transparency. If you lack insight into your data but believe complex relationships and profits exist, machine learning might be suitable for you.
An estimator's point of view
As the results of these calculations are estimators of an underlying value, you need to quantify the variability of their values. For a single concept, choose one or more estimators and stick with them until completely invalidated or validated. Calculate their "variability" and choose the estimators and measures of variability carefully.
Multidimensional pre-treatments
The more independent the characteristics of potentially explanatory variables are, the better they will explain the variables to be explained. However, if you use a statistical method to extract independent variables, remember that the decomposition depends on an estimated parameter. Study the variability/robustness of this parameter at least once.
This parameter must also be calculated in a causal way.
