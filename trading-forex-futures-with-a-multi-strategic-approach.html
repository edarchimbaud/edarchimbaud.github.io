Trading Forex Futures with a Multi-Strategic Approach
A python Backtrader implementation of the algorithmic trading strategy described in the following paper.
Original paper
üìï¬†A Multi Strategy Approach to Trading Foreign Exchange Futures.pdf
Trading rules
Investment Universe: 8 most liquid current month FX futures on CME for AUD, GBP, CAD, EUR, JPY, MXN, NZD, and CHF
Individual Strategies (Indicators):
Interest Rate Carry
Momentum
Mean Reversion
Equity Momentum
Commodity Momentum
Indicator Normalization:
Calculate percentile score (range 0-1)
Subtract 0.5 to remove long bias
Risk Budgeting:
Proportional to absolute value of normalized indicator values
10% annualized total risk
Constant risk target across indicators
Modified optimization for negative weights
Strategy Combination:
Equal weighting
Monthly rebalancing
Why it works
The functionality of this approach is based on a set of normalized working strategies used as indicators to forecast FX futures returns. The carry strategy, equity momentum, and commodity prices, specifically agricultural commodities, oil, and metal, are significant in forecasting futures returns. Cross-currency rates between developed currencies tend to mean revert to a single value. The selected indicator set is crucial for accurate predictions. The multi-strategy approach yields more consistent returns and enhances the Sharpe ratio compared to individual strategies.
Hedging stocks in bear markets?
The source and related research papers do not provide information on the correlation structure of the proposed trading strategy with equity market risk. As a result, it is unclear whether this strategy can be utilized as a hedge or for diversification during times of market crisis.
