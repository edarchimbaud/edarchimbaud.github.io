Seizing Plunging Stocks: A Tactical Approach
A python Backtrader implementation of the algorithmic trading strategy described in the following paper.
Original paper
üìï¬†Tactical allocation in falling stocks: Combining momentum and solvency ratio signals.pdf
Trading rules
Investment Universe: All US stocks excluding illiquid stocks (priced under $0.5) and closed-end funds
Monthly Selection: Choose stocks with 50% or greater losses in the last 500 trading days compared to S&P 500 index
Classification: Rank stocks based on Debt/Equity ratio within their industry
Selection Criteria: Pick stocks with Debt/Equity ratio within the lowest 10% of the industry
Portfolio Rebalancing: Conduct monthly rebalancing
Stock Weighting: Apply equal weight to all stocks in the portfolio
Why it works
The anomaly may be due to investors overreacting to extreme past performance. Behavioral biases influence this reaction. Financial stability factors affect stock selection among falling stocks, as they increase the chances of recovery.
Hedging stocks in bear markets?
Long-only value stocks cannot serve as a hedge against market drops due to their high correlation with the broad equity market. The use of a long-short value factor portfolio as a hedge presents mixed evidence, as value factor definitions vary and their performance differs during stressful times. Research papers, such as Cakici and Tan‚Äôs work, connect value factor premium to liquidity and growth risk, indicating that value factor returns may be low before periods of sluggish global economic growth and poor liquidity.
